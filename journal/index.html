Post 5 - Game Design Research

03/04/2025

The article on best game design ux practices offered great input on how to best design for users interacting with your game content. It suggested practices that I had never really considered when designing for touch screen or mobile games such as areas of the screen that are harder or easier to reach. Placing interactive elements like store buttons, settings, and ads in areas easily reachable by right-handed users is effective for easing the user flow. And implementing features such as sliders and properly-timed pop-ups can help avoid friction from users. One of the main takeaways from this article for me was the recommendation to place buttons or call to action buttons that are good for your game on the right side where they are more accessible to right-handed users. This also helps in preventing data or progress loss if the pop-up is asking something such as to save or delete something.

After reading the article “10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills” article, I found that practicing visual thinking skills to be very beneficial. As seen with students of all ages, it can help with deeper thinking and paying close attention to detail. As designers, specifically UI/UX designers, we get the opportunities to create visual landscapes and environments that users can explore through a website or app. And paying attention to details such as context within what you’re creating and how you can incorporate interactions and images to give off a certain feeling or brand to website/app visitors.

Post 2 - Overlays Design Pattern Research

01/30/2025

The article "Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows" provides important insight on how to best design modal interfaces for users. As designers, it’s important to provide clear options for our users. This includes, taking into account focus, readability, clear navigation, and screen space. A modal should have a title, clearly labeled buttons/navigation, and only appear in response to specific user interactions. The article also gave a great tip that I had never thought about, which was avoiding using modals and overlays on mobile screens since the screen sizes are too small and wouldn’t really work. Also, thinking about accessibility and keyboard navigation as it is common for users to use buttons such as escape to navigate through pages. By following these best practices, we can design better user experiences and reduce annoyances users come across.

Post 1 - Best Practices for Form Design

01/21/2025

After reading the article on best practices for form design, I took away several valuable lessons about putting the user first. As UI/UX designers, we are often designing for a broad audience when creating a public website or form. So, we must take into consideration the most effective ways to maintain user engagement, boost usability and design for accessibility. I have created forms before, so looking over this article served as a checklist for me to see what I was doing a good job with and what I could improve upon. Forms can be tedious to fill out, so by using the practices mentioned in the article, such as providing a number of steps, minimizing input fields, providing feedback and labeling, we can make them easier to complete and straightforward for the user. As well as, thinking about factors that impact accessibility such as color contrast, allowing users to change text size, and designing for screen readers.

As part of a UI/UX internship, I had to interact a lot with Glassdoor's Employer Form for creating an account. I believe they demonstrate best practices for design since they always use labels, differing sizes of boxes for input fields depending on character count, and always giving form feedback. Additionally, their color contrast is made with accessibility in mind and uses primary, secondary, tertiary, and disabled buttons to give additional feedback.
