Grandpa's House

previousnext

During my trip, we stayed at my grandpa's house that he left his family behind in El Rincon, Jalisco. My mom was born in Apatzingan, Michoacan and raised there in their primary family home; since my grandpa owned a welding shop in that town that my uncles now own. And so this second house served as a vacation home to my mom's family growing up as they would come visit family we also have in El Rincon. It's also where my dad was born and raised and actually met my mom during one of her family visits. Luckily, it's a fairly large house and I was able to stay there with a few of my aunts, cousins, and grandma. In Mexico, it is common for people to stay up late and spend time with family. Every night we played a Mexican card game called 'Loteria' and accompanied it with coffee, chatting, and late night snacks that we would often get from corner stores or food vendors that would sell in the town's center plaza. That is the first image, we would gather around the dinner table to hangout and chat every night. This is one of the biggest things I miss about Mexico, and being in a small pueblo like El Rincon. The other pictures are from the outside of the house. Some of them are of me and my cousins, uncle, mom and grandma; and the others are of my mom and her family.

Mazamitla and Zapotlan, Jalisco

previousnext

About an hour from where we were staying in El Rincon was another town called Mazamitla. The name derives from Nahuatl language and is a combination of words meaning "deer" and "place". That's why there are deer related items around the town and in the pictures. Zapotlan was another town we stopped at on the way home that is about 20 minutes from El Rincon. It is a larger, more urban town that has a lot of nice architecture and stores to shop at. The first and last 3 images are taken from Zapotlan.

Dad's Parcela

previousnext

A 'parcela' is plot or plot of land in Spanish, and my dad left one behind before leaving to the U.S. My uncle who visits regularly and also has a house nearby takes care of the land and a few animals he has there for himself. The pictures I took are of the path leading to there that I walked with my uncle, some crops that are nearby, a coconut tree I passed, and some pictures of the land. It's a pretty big piece of land at the base of a large mountain and surrounded by natural environment. I enjoyed visiting it and hope to help my dad do something with it someday.
